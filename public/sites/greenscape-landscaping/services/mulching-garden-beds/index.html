Our Mulching Process

At GreenScape Landscaping, our comprehensive mulching process begins with a thorough assessment of your existing garden beds and plant health. We carefully remove old, decomposed mulch and weeds, then edge your beds to create clean, defined borders that enhance your landscape's appearance. Our team selects the appropriate mulch type based on your specific plants, soil conditions, and aesthetic preferences. We apply mulch at the optimal depth of 2-3 inches, ensuring proper coverage while avoiding common mistakes like mulch volcanoes around trees. Finally, we perform a detailed cleanup, leaving your property pristine and your garden beds perfectly maintained.

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of mulch is best for my garden beds?
The best mulch depends on your plants and aesthetic preferences, but we typically recommend organic options like hardwood mulch or cedar mulch for most Westfield gardens. Our team will assess your specific needs and recommend the ideal mulch type during our consultation.
How often should I have my beds re-mulched?
Most garden beds benefit from fresh mulch application once or twice per year, typically in spring and fall. We'll evaluate your current mulch condition and provide a customized maintenance schedule based on your landscape's specific needs.
Do you remove old mulch before applying new mulch?
Yes, we remove old, decomposed mulch and weeds as part of our comprehensive service to prevent buildup and ensure proper drainage. This preparation step is crucial for maintaining healthy soil conditions and preventing pest issues.
