Our Brake Inspection Process

At Matty's Automotive, our comprehensive brake service begins with a thorough inspection of your entire braking system. Our certified technicians examine brake pads, rotors, calipers, brake lines, and brake fluid to identify any wear, damage, or potential safety concerns. We use precision measuring tools to check rotor thickness and pad wear, ensuring everything meets manufacturer specifications and safety standards. During the inspection, we also test your brake pedal feel and check for any unusual noises or vibrations that could indicate underlying issues. This detailed assessment allows us to provide you with an accurate diagnosis and recommend only the services your vehicle truly needs, whether that's a simple brake pad replacement or more comprehensive brake system repairs.

Why Choose Matty's Automotive

Our brake service includes everything from routine brake pad and rotor replacement to complete brake system overhauls, brake fluid flushes, and emergency brake adjustments. You should bring your vehicle in if you notice squealing or grinding noises when braking, vibrations in the steering wheel or brake pedal, a soft or spongy brake pedal feel, or if your vehicle pulls to one side when braking. We also service brake lines, master cylinders, and ABS systems when needed. Ignoring brake problems can lead to complete brake failure, putting you and your passengers at serious risk, while also causing more expensive damage to rotors, calipers, and other brake components. Our goal is to catch issues early and fix them right the first time, saving you money and ensuring your safety on Springfield's roads.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my brakes inspected?
We recommend having your brakes inspected at least once a year or every 12,000 miles, whichever comes first. However, if you notice any warning signs like squealing, grinding, or changes in brake pedal feel, you should have them checked immediately regardless of when your last inspection was.
How much does brake service typically cost and how long does it take?
Brake service costs vary depending on what needs to be done, but basic brake pad replacement typically takes 1-2 hours, while more comprehensive brake work may require additional time. We always provide upfront estimates before beginning any work, and most brake services can be completed the same day.
What should I expect during my brake service appointment?
We'll start with a complete brake system inspection and explain our findings before recommending any services. You'll receive a detailed estimate, and we'll keep you informed throughout the repair process. After completion, we'll test drive your vehicle and review the work performed with you before you leave.
