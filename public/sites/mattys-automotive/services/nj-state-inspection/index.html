Our Inspection Process

At Matty's Automotive, our New Jersey State Inspection follows a systematic approach to evaluate your vehicle's safety and emissions systems. Our certified inspectors begin with a comprehensive visual examination of your vehicle's exterior, interior, and engine compartment, checking critical safety components like lights, mirrors, windshield wipers, and horn functionality. We then conduct thorough brake system testing, tire tread depth measurement, and suspension component inspection to ensure your vehicle meets all safety standards. For emissions testing, we connect advanced diagnostic equipment to your vehicle's onboard computer system to verify that your car's emission control systems are functioning properly and meeting New Jersey's environmental standards.

What's Included

Our complete NJ State Inspection covers all required safety and emissions components mandated by New Jersey law, including brake system evaluation, steering and suspension checks, exhaust system inspection, and emissions testing for applicable vehicles. You can expect a thorough examination that typically takes 30-45 minutes, during which we'll test your lights, signals, horn, wipers, mirrors, and tire condition. Common signs you need an inspection include an expired inspection sticker, dashboard warning lights, unusual exhaust smoke, or brake performance issues. Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I need a NJ State Inspection?
New Jersey requires annual vehicle inspections for most passenger vehicles. Your inspection sticker will display the month your next inspection is due, and you can get inspected up to two months before your expiration date.
How much does a NJ State Inspection cost?
The standard NJ State Inspection fee is set by the state and typically takes 30-45 minutes to complete. We recommend calling ahead to schedule your appointment and confirm current pricing and availability.
What should I bring for my inspection?
Please bring your vehicle registration, insurance card, and any previous inspection documentation. Make sure your gas tank is between 1/4 and 3/4 full, and ensure all lights and basic systems are functioning before arrival.
